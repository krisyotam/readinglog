My Reading Process: Grammar, Logic, Rhetoric

My approach to reading is built around the three classic stages of classical education: Grammar, Logic, and Rhetoric.

Grammar: Understanding the basics—what the text says, defining the terms, and interpreting the structure of the argument.

Logic: Analyzing the text, identifying premises and conclusions, and evaluating the soundness of the argument.

Rhetoric: Engaging with the text persuasively, forming my own stance, and communicating my understanding effectively.

Why & How I Read 7 Books at a Time

I read seven books simultaneously to engage with a variety of subjects, ensuring I’m not hindered or rushed by the complexities of one topic. Each book represents a different area of learning, and I alternate between them to maintain variety and stimulate different parts of my intellect. This method allows me to sustain a dynamic and comprehensive approach to knowledge. P.S. This does not include textbooks or research articles.

  • Mathematics: Deepening my understanding of abstract concepts and exploring new mathematical landscapes.
  • Physics: Delving into the laws that govern the natural world, from the smallest particles to the vast universe.
  • Pedagogy: Enhancing my teaching strategies and deepening my understanding of how knowledge is best imparted.
  • Philosophy: Contemplating life's fundamental questions and exploring the nature of knowledge, existence, and morality.
  • Classic Literature: Immersing myself in timeless works that have shaped the human experience and culture.
  • Fiction: Enjoying psychological thrillers, murder mysteries, and other suspense-filled narratives that captivate the imagination.

Special Sections

In addition to my main seven books, I also dedicate time to the following categories, which serve a different purpose:

  • Poetry: Quick reads that fuel my creativity as a writer and poet, providing inspiration through emotional depth and linguistic beauty.
  • Children’s Books: Short, engaging stories that offer insight into how narratives shape young minds and help refine my approach to teaching and education.

About Me

I'm Kris Yotam, an enthusiast of learning, mathematics, and the pursuit of knowledge. My mission is to integrate the wisdom of the past with the insights of the present to shape a deeper understanding of the world. I am particularly passionate about self-education and classical methods of learning, and I apply these methods across a variety of subjects in my daily reading and research.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I am developing curricula to help others learn in ways that foster critical thinking and a love for learning. My long-term goal is to share this knowledge through teaching and mentoring, helping others to grow and achieve their full potential.
